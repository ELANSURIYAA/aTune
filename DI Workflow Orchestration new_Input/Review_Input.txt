- Check that all deduplicated and merged tasks from the input are mapped to an agent or explicitly marked as a gap.
- Validate that all dependencies, triggers, and handoff points described in the agent definition cards are captured in the workflow sequence.
- Confirm that all business rules, compliance requirements, and audit trail specifications from the input are included in the workflow recommendations.
- Ensure that all gap/missing item handling requirements, including escalation and ambiguity resolution, are covered as per both the instructions and input files.
- Verify that all required classifications ([INPUT], [INFERRED], [RECOMMENDED]) are included or referenced where applicable.
- Confirm that all referenced files, tables, and agent mapping relationships are analyzed and integrated where required.
- Ensure that all open items and gaps listed in the input are either addressed or explicitly tracked in the output.
- Check that all required validations, success criteria, and KPIs from the agent cards are included in the workflow or recommendations.
- Validate that all use cases, scenarios, and manual intervention points from the Task-Agent Mapping are covered.
- Confirm that all formatting and structural requirements from the instructions are followed, including bullet point minimums and section ordering.
- Ensure that no relevant section or entity from the input files is omitted or ignored.
- Verify that all required tables, mappings, and relationships are generated as specified.

Accuracy Checks

- Confirm that the output aligns strictly with the agent instructions and does not introduce unsupported assumptions or agent names.
- Validate that all agent selections and workflow steps are justified by the input Task-Agent Mapping and Consolidated Thinking Document.
- Ensure that all dependency mappings and technical relationships are logically correct and traceable to the input content.
- Check that all gap identifications and recommendations are justified by the actual gaps in the input files.
- Verify that all classifications ([INPUT], [INFERRED], [RECOMMENDED]) are used accurately and only where supported by the input.
- Confirm that all business rule interpretations and compliance requirements are represented as per the input, with no omissions or hallucinations.
- Ensure that all findings, recommendations, and escalation points are traceable to either the instructions or the input files.
- Validate that all terminology, agent names, and workflow steps are used consistently and match those in the Agent Mapper Table and input documents.
- Check that all required formatting standards, such as bullet point length and section structure, are followed exactly.
- Ensure that no false-positive gaps or unsupported new agents are introduced.
- Confirm that no false-negative omissions occur for any required task, agent, or rule.
- Validate that all values, tables, and classifications accurately match the source content and are not invented or altered.
- Ensure that all relationships between entities, agents, and workflow steps are correctly interpreted and represented.
- Confirm that all status, resolution, and escalation tracking is accurate and instruction-aligned.
- Validate that recommendations and improvements align with the identified findings and input requirements.
- Ensure that no instruction is misinterpreted or partially implemented.

Efficiency Checks

- Verify that duplicate analysis, redundant explanations, and repeated gap entries are avoided throughout the output.
- Confirm that the output remains concise, well-structured, and free of unnecessary sections or verbosity.
- Ensure that duplicate dependency mappings, agent listings, and recommendations are eliminated.
- Check that similar findings and recommendations are logically grouped and not repeated across sections.
- Validate that tables, mappings, and workflow steps are not unnecessarily repeated or verbose.
- Confirm that the output is token-efficient while maintaining all required detail and completeness.
- Ensure that repeated content from the input files is minimized unless explicitly required by the instructions.
- Validate that all findings, recommendations, and classifications are presented only once per relevant context.
- Confirm that large sections are summarized efficiently where allowed, without loss of required detail.
- Ensure that the output structure improves readability and enables efficient review by section and topic.
- Check that redundant validations, checks, or classifications are not generated.
- Validate that output formatting remains clean, optimized, and instruction-compliant throughout.
