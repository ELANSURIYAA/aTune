Option 1: Conceptual Data Model Development Workflow
-----------------------------------------------------

1. Agents to consider:
   • Fabric Model Conceptual (Score: 95-98 for entity/relationship/domain modeling)
   • Fabric Model Data Constraints (Score: 90 for attributes and business rules)

2. What we will get:
   • A complete conceptual entity-relationship model for credit card reporting that includes all core business entities (Application, Campaign, Credit Risk, Fraud Screening, Transaction, Funnel Stage) with their primary keys, attributes, and relationships, derived directly from the credit card reporting requirements document. The Fabric Model Conceptual agent will analyze reporting requirements to define business goals, KPIs, use cases, and produce a conceptual ER view with entity definitions and relationship mappings, achieving a mapping score of 95-98 for entity and relationship modeling tasks.
   
   • A comprehensive set of data constraints, business rules, and attribute-level specifications for each entity in the conceptual model, including validation rules, calculation logic (e.g., approval rates, conversion metrics), data retention policies, security access rules, and audit trail requirements. The Fabric Model Data Constraints agent will document all data expectations, logical constraints, and business rules with a mapping score of 90, ensuring that attributes are governed by explicit business logic and compliance requirements.
   
   • A validated conceptual data model that supports all five credit card reporting requirements (Application Funnel Analysis, Campaign Performance Tracking, Credit Risk Assessment, Fraud Detection Monitoring, Transaction Analysis) with documented entities, attributes, relationships, and business rules, ready for handoff to logical and physical data model implementation teams. The model will include traceability from requirements to entities, documented assumptions, and a validation report confirming 100% coverage of stated reporting objectives.

3. What we will miss:
   • Automated requirements parsing and validation capabilities that extract, structure, and validate the completeness of the credit card reporting requirements document before modeling begins. Without a dedicated Requirements Parsing & Validation Agent, the Fabric Model Conceptual agent must manually interpret the requirements document, which increases the risk of missing sections, ambiguous requirements, or incomplete data elements going undetected until later stages. This gap means there is no systematic validation of document completeness, no structured escalation mechanism for missing or conflicting requirements, and no audit trail of requirements coverage before modeling starts.
   
   • Systematic ambiguity resolution and gap escalation mechanisms that identify, document, and escalate unclear entity definitions, missing attributes, or conflicting business rules during the modeling process. While the Fabric Model Conceptual and Fabric Model Data Constraints agents can produce entities and rules, they lack explicit human-in-the-loop triggers for ambiguity (confidence < 0.8), structured escalation workflows to domain experts or stakeholders, and formal handoff packages that document unresolved items. This means ambiguous requirements may be interpreted inconsistently, critical gaps may not be flagged for stakeholder review, and the final model may contain undocumented assumptions that impact downstream implementation.
   
   • End-to-end model synthesis, integration, and governance validation capabilities that assemble all modeling outputs (entities, attributes, relationships, rules) into a single validated conceptual data model and integrate it with enterprise data governance systems. Without a Model Synthesis & Integration Agent, there is no automated mechanism to cross-validate the outputs of the two available agents, ensure consistency across all entities and rules, integrate the model with data governance repositories, or produce a comprehensive handoff package with validation reports, integration logs, and compliance documentation. This gap increases the risk of incomplete model handoff, failed governance integration, and lack of traceability for audit and compliance purposes.

| Boundary definition | Requirements Parsing & Validation Agent must NOT alter, interpret, or invent any content from the source requirements document; must NOT proceed with modeling if more than 3 critical sections are missing; must NOT resolve ambiguities without stakeholder escalation. Business Rules & Compliance Agent must NOT omit any compliance requirement mentioned in the requirements document; must NOT invent business rules or compliance standards; must NOT approve the model if any compliance requirement is missing or unvalidated. Model Synthesis & Integration Agent must NOT hand off an incomplete model (missing entities, attributes, or relationships); must NOT bypass governance validation; must NOT proceed if integration with governance systems fails. |
| Known issues log    | Requirements Parsing & Validation Agent should log and avoid: (1) false positives in gap detection (e.g., flagging optional sections as missing), (2) failure to detect implicit requirements buried in narrative text, (3) over-escalation of minor ambiguities that can be resolved through standard interpretation. Entity & Attribute Modeling Agent should log and avoid: (1) creating duplicate entities with different names for the same business concept (e.g., "Customer" vs. "Cardholder"), (2) missing implicit relationships between entities (e.g., Application → Campaign relationship implied by campaign tracking requirements), (3) defining attributes without considering downstream logical/physical constraints (e.g., PII attributes requiring encryption). Business Rules & Compliance Agent should log and avoid: (1) missing implicit compliance requirements (e.g., PII handling rules implied by GDPR references), (2) conflicting business rules that are not escalated for resolution, (3) incomplete audit trail specifications (e.g., missing retention period or access control). Model Synthesis & Integration Agent should log and avoid: (1) integration failures due to governance system downtime or API errors, (2) incomplete handoff packages missing validation reports or audit logs, (3) inconsistencies between entity definitions from different upstream agents (e.g., Entity & Attribute Modeling Agent vs. Fabric Model Conceptual). |
