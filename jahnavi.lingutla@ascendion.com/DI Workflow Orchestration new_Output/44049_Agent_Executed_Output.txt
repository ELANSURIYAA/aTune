SECTION 1 — WITH AVAILABLE AGENTS

Option 1 : Conceptual Data Model Development Pipeline

  1. Agents to consider : Fabric Model Conceptual → Fabric Model Data Constraints

  2. What we will get :

     • A complete conceptual data model for credit card reporting will be produced, covering all five required reports (Application Funnel, Campaign Performance, Credit Risk, Fraud Screening, and Transaction Analysis). The Fabric Model Conceptual agent will analyze the requirements document to identify and define all core business entities (Application, Campaign, Credit Risk, Fraud Screening, Transaction, Funnel Stage), their attributes, and the relationships between them, ensuring full coverage of the reporting objectives stated in the requirements.

     • Business goals, KPIs, and use cases for credit card reporting will be explicitly documented as part of the conceptual modeling process. The agent will extract and structure the business understanding layer from the requirements document, including the purpose of each report, the key performance indicators to be tracked (e.g., application conversion rates, campaign ROI, fraud detection rates), and the analytical use cases that the model must support, providing a clear business justification for every entity and relationship in the model.

     • Data constraints, business rules, and validation logic will be defined and documented for all entities and attributes in the conceptual model. The Fabric Model Data Constraints agent will process the conceptual model output to extract and formalize all business rules (e.g., calculation rules for funnel stages, credit risk scoring logic, fraud screening thresholds), data quality expectations (e.g., mandatory fields, valid value ranges, referential integrity rules), and compliance requirements (e.g., PII handling, data retention policies, audit trail specifications), ensuring that the conceptual model is not only structurally complete but also operationally enforceable.

  3. What we will miss :

     • Formal domain-bounded context modeling and enterprise domain architecture alignment will not be explicitly produced by the available agents. While the Fabric Model Conceptual agent identifies entities and relationships, it does not perform explicit domain-driven design (DDD) analysis to define bounded contexts, domain boundaries, or integration points with other enterprise domains (e.g., customer master data, product catalog, financial systems). This gap means that the conceptual model may lack the architectural context needed to ensure consistency and interoperability with other enterprise data models and systems.

     • A structured subject area catalog and formal business glossary will not be automatically generated by the available agents. Although the Fabric Model Conceptual agent lists entities, attributes, and common data elements, it does not produce a formal information architecture artifact such as a subject area hierarchy (e.g., Customer, Product, Transaction, Risk) or a governed business glossary with standardized definitions, synonyms, and ownership metadata. This gap means that business users and downstream teams may lack a shared, governed vocabulary for interpreting and using the conceptual model, increasing the risk of misalignment and inconsistent terminology across reporting and analytics initiatives.

     • Manual validation and sign-off workflows for business goals, domain relationships, and business rules are not automated or orchestrated by the available agents. The agents produce the conceptual model and constraints as outputs, but they do not include built-in human-in-the-loop checkpoints for business stakeholder validation of goals and KPIs, enterprise architect review of domain relationships, information architect structuring of subject areas, data owner approval of business rules, or data governance sign-off on compliance requirements. This gap means that critical validation and approval steps must be managed manually outside the agent workflow, increasing the risk of incomplete or unapproved model artifacts being handed off to downstream implementation teams.

SECTION 2 — WITH AVAILABLE AND NEW AGENTS

Option 1 : Full Conceptual Data Model Development Pipeline with Governance and Validation

  1. Agents to consider : 
     
     Requirements Parsing & Validation Agent [NEW: extracts, validates, and prepares credit card reporting requirements for downstream modeling; escalates missing or ambiguous requirements to stakeholders before modeling begins]

     → Entity & Attribute Modeling Agent [NEW: defines, documents, and validates all entities, attributes, and relationships; resolves entity ambiguity and ensures 100% coverage of requirements] 
     → Business Rules & Compliance Agent [NEW: extracts, documents, and validates all business rules, compliance requirements, security access rules, data retention policies, and audit trail specifications] 
     → Fabric Model Data Constraints 
     → Model Synthesis & Integration Agent [NEW: assembles, validates, and prepares the complete conceptual data model for handoff; integrates with data governance systems and ensures model completeness before downstream handoff]

  2. What we will get :

     • A fully validated and complete conceptual data model will be delivered with guaranteed coverage of all credit card reporting requirements across all five reports. The Requirements Parsing & Validation Agent will ensure that the requirements document is complete, unambiguous, and validated before any modeling begins, eliminating the risk of downstream rework due to missing or unclear requirements. The Entity & Attribute Modeling Agent will then systematically define every entity, attribute, and relationship required by the validated requirements, with explicit escalation and resolution of any ambiguity, ensuring that the conceptual model is comprehensive, accurate, and traceable back to the source requirements.

     • All business rules, compliance requirements, security policies, data retention policies, and audit trail specifications will be extracted, documented, and validated as formal artifacts integrated into the conceptual model. The Business Rules & Compliance Agent will process the requirements document to identify and formalize all business logic (e.g., funnel stage calculations, credit risk scoring rules, fraud detection thresholds), compliance mandates (e.g., PII handling, regulatory reporting requirements), access control policies (e.g., role-based access to sensitive data), retention policies (e.g., transaction history retention periods), and audit requirements (e.g., change tracking, lineage documentation). These artifacts will be validated by compliance officers and data governance teams before being integrated into the final model, ensuring that the conceptual model is not only structurally sound but also fully compliant and operationally enforceable.

     • The complete conceptual data model will be assembled, validated, and integrated with enterprise data governance systems before handoff to downstream implementation teams. The Model Synthesis & Integration Agent will consolidate all outputs from the upstream agents (entities, attributes, relationships, business rules, compliance requirements, constraints) into a single, validated conceptual model package. It will perform completeness checks to ensure that all requirements are covered, validate the model against data governance standards and policies, integrate the model metadata into the enterprise data catalog and governance platform, and produce a formal handoff package (including the conceptual ER diagram, entity/attribute specifications, business rules catalog, compliance documentation, and validation reports) that is ready for logical and physical data model implementation. This end-to-end validation and integration ensures that the conceptual model is not only complete and accurate but also approved, governed, and ready for downstream use without requiring manual rework or re-validation.

     Fabric Model Conceptual

     • A structured subject area catalog and formal business glossary will be produced as governed information architecture artifacts. The Domain Architecture & Glossary Agent will organize the conceptual entities into a hierarchical subject area structure (e.g., Customer, Product, Transaction, Risk, Compliance), define each subject area's scope and ownership, and produce a formal business glossary that includes standardized definitions, synonyms, related terms, data stewards, and usage guidelines for every entity, attribute, and business term in the conceptual model. The Fabric Model Data Constraints agent will process the conceptual model and glossary outputs to extract and formalize all business rules, data quality expectations, and compliance requirements, producing a complete set of constraints and validation logic that can be implemented in downstream logical and physical data models. This ensures that the conceptual model is not only structurally complete and architecturally aligned but also includes the business logic and validation rules needed to ensure data quality, compliance, and operational accuracy in the implemented reporting solution.
