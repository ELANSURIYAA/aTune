SECTION 4 — IMPROVEMENTS & OPTIMISATIONS

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACCURACY — How well the agent produces correct, on-target
outputs and avoids errors.

| Criteria            | Description                          |
|---------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Self-validation     | **Requirements Parsing & Validation Agent**: Re-validate parsed requirements against the original document to ensure no sections were missed or misinterpreted; cross-check that all mandatory sections (entities, attributes, relationships, business rules, compliance) are present and structured correctly before passing to downstream agents. **Fabric Model Conceptual**: Cross-reference all identified entities and relationships against the parsed requirements to ensure 100% traceability; validate that no entities are invented or omitted. **Entity & Attribute Modeling Agent**: Re-validate all attributes and relationships against source requirements and business process documentation to ensure completeness and accuracy; check that all mandatory attributes are defined and no unsupported relationships are assumed. **Business Rules & Compliance Agent**: Cross-check all extracted business rules and compliance requirements against regulatory standards (PCI-DSS, GDPR, CCPA) to ensure no rules are missed or misinterpreted. **Model Synthesis & Integration Agent**: Validate the assembled conceptual model against the original requirements document to ensure 100% coverage of all entities, attributes, relationships, rules, and compliance requirements before handoff. |
| Boundary definition | **Requirements Parsing & Validation Agent**: Must NOT alter, paraphrase, or interpret the source requirements; must NOT proceed with modeling if critical sections are missing; must NOT invent or assume missing information. **Fabric Model Conceptual**: Must NOT create entities or relationships that are not explicitly stated or clearly implied in the requirements; must NOT omit required entities or attributes; must NOT assume business logic without evidence. **Entity & Attribute Modeling Agent**: Must NOT invent attributes or relationships without traceability to source requirements; must NOT resolve ambiguities without stakeholder consultation; must NOT skip mandatory attributes. **Business Rules & Compliance Agent**: Must NOT omit compliance requirements; must NOT invent business rules; must NOT approve incomplete or conflicting rule sets without escalation. **Model Synthesis & Integration Agent**: Must NOT hand off incomplete models; must NOT bypass validation or governance integration steps; must NOT proceed if integration with governance systems fails. |
| Known issues log    | **Requirements Parsing & Validation Agent**: Log all instances of missing sections, ambiguous language, conflicting statements, or incomplete specifications detected during parsing; track escalation response times and resolution outcomes to improve future parsing logic. **Fabric Model Conceptual**: Log all cases where entity definitions are unclear, relationships are ambiguous, or attributes are missing from requirements; track patterns of requirement gaps to inform requirements template improvements. **Entity & Attribute Modeling Agent**: Log all ambiguity escalations, missing attribute incidents, and relationship conflicts; track resolution patterns to build a knowledge base of common modeling challenges and their resolutions. **Business Rules & Compliance Agent**: Log all instances of conflicting business rules, missing compliance requirements, or unclear regulatory language; track compliance escalations and resolutions to build a compliance pattern library. **Model Synthesis & Integration Agent**: Log all integration failures, validation errors, and incomplete model handoff attempts; track root causes to improve upstream agent outputs and integration logic. |

| Define task breakdown     | **Requirements Parsing & Validation Agent**: Break down into subtasks: (1) Extract entities section, (2) Extract attributes section, (3) Extract relationships section, (4) Extract business rules section, (5) Extract compliance section, (6) Validate completeness, (7) Escalate gaps. **Fabric Model Conceptual**: Break down into subtasks: (1) Identify core entities, (2) Define entity attributes, (3) Establish entity relationships, (4) Document modeling rationale, (5) Validate model completeness. **Entity & Attribute Modeling Agent**: Break down into subtasks: (1) Parse entity definitions from requirements, (2) Map attributes to entities, (3) Define attribute data types and constraints, (4) Establish relationships with cardinality, (5) Detect and escalate ambiguities, (6) Validate completeness. **Business Rules & Compliance Agent**: Break down into subtasks: (1) Extract business rules, (2) Extract compliance requirements, (3) Define access policies, (4) Define retention policies, (5) Define audit trail specifications, (6) Validate rule completeness, (7) Escalate conflicts. **Model Synthesis & Integration Agent**: Break down into subtasks: (1) Assemble all model components, (2) Validate model completeness, (3) Integrate with governance systems, (4) Generate validation report, (5) Prepare handoff documentation, (6) Escalate integration failures. |

COST EFFECTIVE — How to minimise cost per agent without
sacrificing quality.

| Criteria                       | Description               |
|--------------------------------|---------------------------|
| Low cost model routing         | **Requirements Parsing & Validation Agent**: Use GPT-4o for initial parsing and ambiguity detection (high complexity); route completeness validation and gap logging to GPT-4o-mini (rule-based, low complexity). **Fabric Model Conceptual**: Use GPT-4o for entity identification and relationship establishment (high reasoning complexity); route attribute data type assignment and primary key definition to GPT-4o-mini (rule-based, low complexity). **Entity & Attribute Modeling Agent**: Use GPT-4o for ambiguity resolution and relationship validation (high reasoning complexity); route attribute mapping and data type assignment to GPT-4o-mini (rule-based, low complexity). **Business Rules & Compliance Agent**: Use Claude Sonnet 3.5 for compliance requirement extraction and rule conflict detection (high precision required); route access policy and retention policy documentation to GPT-4o-mini (rule-based, low complexity). **Model Synthesis & Integration Agent**: Use GPT-4o-mini for model assembly, validation report generation, and handoff documentation (structured output, low reasoning complexity); escalate integration failures to GPT-4o for root cause analysis (high reasoning complexity). |
| Token size reduction           | **Requirements Parsing & Validation Agent**: Summarize or chunk large requirements documents into sections (entities, attributes, relationships, rules, compliance) and process each section independently; cache common regulatory standards (PCI-DSS, GDPR, CCPA) to avoid re-sending in every call. **Fabric Model Conceptual**: Remove verbose requirement descriptions and retain only entity names, attribute lists, and relationship statements in the input context; cache data modeling standards and naming conventions to avoid re-sending. **Entity & Attribute Modeling Agent**: Trim input context to include only entity definitions, attribute lists, and relationship statements; remove all narrative text and examples; cache business process documentation and reference it by ID rather than including full text. **Business Rules & Compliance Agent**: Extract only business rule statements and compliance requirement text from the requirements document; remove all explanatory text and examples; cache regulatory standards and reference them by ID. **Model Synthesis & Integration Agent**: Receive only structured JSON outputs from upstream agents (entities, attributes, relationships, rules, compliance) rather than full narrative reports; cache governance policy repository and reference policies by ID rather than including full text. |
| Hybrid model                   | **Requirements Parsing & Validation Agent**: Use regex and rule-based parsing to extract section headers, entity names, and attribute lists from structured requirements documents; use LLM only for ambiguity detection and gap analysis. **Fabric Model Conceptual**: Use rule-based logic to assign standard data types (e.g., string, integer, date) to attributes based on naming conventions (e.g., "ID" → integer, "Date" → date); use LLM only for entity identification and relationship establishment. **Entity & Attribute Modeling Agent**: Use rule-based logic to validate attribute completeness (e.g., check that all entities have primary keys, all attributes have data types); use LLM only for ambiguity resolution and relationship validation. **Business Rules & Compliance Agent**: Use rule-based lookups to match compliance requirements to standard regulatory frameworks (PCI-DSS, GDPR, CCPA) by keyword; use LLM only for rule conflict detection and escalation. **Model Synthesis & Integration Agent**: Use rule-based validation to check model completeness (e.g., all entities have attributes, all relationships have cardinality); use API calls for governance system integration; use LLM only for validation report generation and escalation analysis. |
